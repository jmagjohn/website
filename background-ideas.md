# Background Surface Ideas

Ideas for replacing or evolving the undulating wireframe wave.

---

## 1. Synthwave Grid (Retro)
Classic 80s perspective grid with a flat horizon line. Glows pink and cyan. The lines pulse outward like a heartbeat. Sun setting in the background optional but highly recommended. Very Outrun. Very cringe. Perfect.

## 2. Lava Lamp (Smooth)
Slow-moving metaball blobs that merge and separate in dark purples and magentas. No wireframe, just pure soft gradients. Feels like screensaver art from 2003 but make it expensive.

## 3. Plasma (Psychedelic)
The classic sin/cos plasma effect from old demoscene intros. Every pixel shifts through the full rainbow based on overlapping sine waves. Loud. Nauseating if you stare too long. Amazing.

## 4. Holographic Foil (Smooth / Iridescent)
A flat surface that shifts between rainbow interference colors as it slowly ripples. Like the inside of a DVD or a shiny sticker. No edges, just color. Very 2020 streetwear energy.

## 5. Circuit Board (Retro / Tech)
Dark background with glowing traces routing around like a PCB. Packets of light travel along the paths. Looks like you built something, even if you didn't. Very hacker. Very fake hacker.

## 6. Deep Bioluminescence (Smooth / Dark)
Almost pitch black ocean floor. Tiny particles drift upward, pulsing soft blue-green like deep sea creatures. Slow. Meditative. Nobody knows what's down there.

## 7. Quantum Foam (Psychedelic / BS Science)
Rapid tiny bubbles of spacetime constantly forming and popping across the surface. Iridescent. References real theoretical physics if anyone asks. They won't. Sounds cool though.

## 8. Coral Reef (Organic / Colorful)
Swaying organic shapes in warm coral pinks, teals, and yellows. Animated like they're underwater. Zero reason to put this on a portfolio site. Would be insane. Do it.

## 9. Starfield Warp (Retro / Space)
Stars streaming toward the viewer from a central vanishing point. Classic screensaver energy. Speed up on scroll. Hit warp 9. Engage.

## 10. Molten Glass (Smooth / Weird)
A slowly churning surface that looks like the top of a lava flow or cooling glass. Dark reds, oranges, and blacks. Cracks form and heal. Uncomfortable to look at but impossible to stop.
